Visual Hierarchy

Squarespace

https://www.squarespace.com/

As part of Squarespaces's buisness model is to provide a framework to design stunning websites, it's no surprise that their own homepage is beautifully designed and includes many good design principles. I specifically chose to focus on visual hierarchy because it was immediately clear what I was supposed to focus on when I opened the page. I first read the title and then jumped directly to the button. From there I transitioned to the examples in the bottom right and finished by reading the heading under the title. This same type of design also occurs when scrolling down, directly leading my eyes through the hierarchy they created. I really liked their design in general for its simplicity and ability to lead my eye in a logical way without having to directly spell it out.

Hick's Law

McMaster-Carr

https://www.mcmaster.com/

In many ways, McMaster-Carr's web design is dated and non-responsive, however there is one element in which I believe they excel. McMaster-Carr sells over 550,000 items, and yet to narrow down a search is extremely easy. Despite having many options, none of them are really comperable in a way that requires much thought. In addition, each category, even going down to individual items, has pictures and descriptions to help users find what they need. Once an initial category is selected, the filter menu on the left is extremely useful in helping narrow down what is needed. Many of the filters also have illustrations or descriptions which also aid the user. I think the design overall is especially suited for McMaster-Carr's userbase, who tend to be purpose driven and already know what they are looking for, only needing to find it.

Repetition

Wikipedia

https://www.wikipedia.org/

Wikipedias's design is simple and repetative. Whether on their home page or countless articles, their design repeats colors, styles, and layouts frequently. This repetition makes it extremely easy to navigate through pages to find relevent information. Their style and repetition leads to a very recognizable site. Even on their home page there are nine repeated elements, with each one changing only the language it is displayed in. Commonly repeating elements of a wikipedia page include text formatting, the table of contents, fact boxes on the right of the screen, and the menu on the left. Overall, Wikipedia's design is extremely repetative, which helps to make them recognizeable and easy to navigate.
